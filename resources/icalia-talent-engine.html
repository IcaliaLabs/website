01 — Source

Sourcing never stops. It's part of the value.

We don't start a search when you send a job description — we're in the market every day, mapping talent across Latin America and building relationships before positions open.

Multi-channel, always on

LinkedIn, GitHub and open-source communities, active headhunting, inbound applicants, and referrals from our own engineer network — each channel weighted by signal quality.

A regional capability map

Years of placements taught us where specific strengths live: security and DevOps hubs, Ruby and Python strongholds, the best English-proficiency markets. We know where to start looking, per stack.

Your timezone, by design

All engineers work in U.S.-overlapping time zones. Daily standups, real-time pairing, and same-day answers — the core reason clients move work nearshore.

AI-indexed talent database

Strong candidates we've already interviewed — but not yet placed — live in an internal, AI-searchable database. That's how a two-week staffing timeline is possible: we rarely start from zero.

02 — Vet

We don't trust a résumé until we've proven the person behind it.

AI has made fake profiles cheap: industry leaders estimate that a large share of technical applicants today are fraudulent or misrepresented. Our pipeline is built for that reality.

1

Fraud-aware screening

Every résumé is cross-checked against real social proof — LinkedIn history, GitHub contributions, public work — using internal tooling that flags profiles that don't correlate with claimed experience.

2

Identity verification

Before any client-facing interview, candidates pass a formal ID check through an international verification vendor: document scanning, database validation, and tamper detection. Criminal background checks are available for regulated engagements.

3

Technical evaluation

Mid-level means 4–6 years of hands-on experience; senior means 6+. Beyond years, we specifically evaluate AI-native ability — engineers who use models, copilots, and agentic tooling to multiply their output, not just talk about it.

4

Recorded English assessment

Every engineer must articulate architecture and engineering decisions fluently in English. We record a video assessment before a profile ever reaches your inbox — the bar is binary: client-ready or not presented.

5

The hire-bar gate

The final filter is our own: "Would we hire this person for our team?" If the answer isn't a confident yes, the profile doesn't move forward. When a candidate reaches you, our name is on it.

03 — Hire

Clean employment mechanics. Zero cross-border headaches for you.

You get one U.S. counterpart and one contract. We absorb the complexity of hiring across Latin American jurisdictions.

U.S.-entity employment

Engineers are engaged through our U.S. entity and paid in USD — a strong retention lever for top LatAm talent, and a clean structure for you.

IP protected under U.S. law

IP assignment, confidentiality, and invention agreements are executed under American jurisdiction, structured with cross-border legal and accounting counsel.

Flexible integration

Engineers embed in your structure and can operate under your credentials and brand — full white-label if needed — or ours. SOWs adapt to your industry's compliance requirements.

No bench, by design

We don't warehouse idle engineers and pass you the cost. Each search draws from live sourcing plus our pre-vetted database — matched to your stack, seniority, and culture.

2–4 weeks to staff

With a clear picture of your stack, project, and evaluation criteria, we present candidates in as little as two weeks. Clarity is the accelerator — we run a structured intake to get it fast.

A bar you help set

The technical bar is calibrated with you — we've staffed teams held to San Francisco-level engineering standards and adjusted our process to match.

04 — Onboard

Secure and productive from day one.

Onboarding is a process we own — equipment, policies, security training, and a named team around every engineer.

Equipment we provision

We supply and control the engineer's machine, honoring the IP and confidentiality agreements in place — with your tooling and licenses layered on top.

Security & policy training

Every engagement starts with our standard onboarding: security practices, acceptable-use policies, and signed policy acknowledgments — applied even when your industry doesn't require it yet. Client-specific courses are added on request.

Audit-ready documentation

Contractor agreements incorporate your standards (HIPAA and similar sign-offs where relevant). Policies, trainings, and acknowledgments are documented and refreshed on a quarterly cadence.

05 — Beyond Day One

Placement is the beginning, not the finish line.

A structured quality loop keeps every engagement healthy — and surfaces issues before they become problems.

Dual feedback loops

Weekly or biweekly checkpoints with you, plus parallel 1:1s with each engineer covering engagement, attitude, wellbeing, and technical direction.

Green / yellow / red early warning

We track signals before they become red flags. If performance dips, a remediation plan starts immediately — and a replacement is quietly prepared in parallel, so you're never exposed.

Quarterly enablement

Engineers receive ongoing touchpoints on security practices and what's moving in your industry — fintech, healthcare, logistics — so their thinking stays current, not just their code.
